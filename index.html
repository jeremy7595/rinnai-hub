Error Codes

Diagnostic Quick Reference

The codes you'll actually see and where to start. Applies broadly across Rinnai tankless — always confirm against the unit's service manual before replacing parts.

CodeWhat it meansFirst checks in the field
10Air supply / exhaust blockageWalk the vent — termination screen, bird nests, sag in horizontal runs. Check the fan blade for debris and confirm vent length is within spec.
11No ignitionConfirm gas is on and supply is adequate. Check inlet gas pressure under load, igniter spark, and gas solenoids. Verify gas type matches the rating plate.
12Flame failureLow gas pressure under load is the classic cause — check it at max fire. Inspect the flame rod and its connection, look for exhaust recirculating into the intake, verify the ground wire.
14Thermal fuse / overheat wrapUsually scale in the heat exchanger or restricted airflow. Descale and find the cause before replacing the fuse — it tripped for a reason.
16Over-temperature warningScale buildup or restricted flow — flush the heat exchanger, clean the inlet filter, verify flow control operation.
19Electrical grounding faultMoisture in connectors, chafed wiring, or a sensor shorted to the case. Verify a solid dedicated ground.
25Condensate trap / drain blocked (condensing)Clear the trap and drain line, check neutralizer media, confirm drain slope. Never cap a condensate line.
31Burner sensor faultCheck the connector and sensor resistance; replace the sensor if open or shorted.
32Outgoing water temp sensorCheck the connector, sensor resistance, and scale on the probe. Replace if out of spec.
33Heat exchanger temp sensorSame drill as 32 — connector, resistance, scale.
34Combustion air temp sensorFirst check for exhaust re-entering the intake (vent termination clearances), then the sensor and fan.
52Modulating gas valve faultCheck the valve connector and coil resistance; replace the modulating valve if out of spec.
61Combustion fan failureSpin the fan by hand — it should be free. Check motor resistance and the connector; replace the fan motor.
65Water flow control valve faultServo jammed or scaled — clean or replace the flow control assembly.
71Gas solenoid valve circuitCheck solenoid coils and wiring; if the coils test good, suspect the PCB.
72Flame sensor faultFlame signal with no ignition sequence — dirty or shorted flame rod, or the PCB.
79Water leak detected in cabinetFind and fix the leak (heat exchanger, fittings, valves), dry the leak sensor, then clear the code.
LCScale buildup in heat exchangerDescale now — LC is the unit telling you it's limed up. Check hardness and recommend treatment; ignored LCs end in a 14 or 16.

No code and no power at all? Breaker, 120V at the receptacle, and the fuses on the PCB — in that order.

Flow Rate vs Temperature Rise (ΔT)

Max hot water is physics: GPM = (BTU × efficiency) ÷ (500 × ΔT). ΔT = set temp − incoming water temp. "Not enough hot water" in January is usually just a bigger ΔT, not a broken unit.

Dip Switch & Parameter Settings

Kill power before touching switches. Set only what the manual tells you to — everything else stays factory.

ElevationSW 2SW 3
0 – 2,000 ft (factory)OFFOFF
2,001 – 5,400 ftONOFF
5,401 – 7,700 ft (Big Bear, Wrightwood)OFFON
7,701 – 10,200 ftONON

Altitude switches 2 & 3 live in the tan DIPSW1 bank on the front of the PCB. Once set, no gas pressure re-adjustment is needed for altitude. Vent-length setting: RE/REP use controller parameter 99 — factory 99A for long runs, change to 99b only when total vent is under 21 equivalent ft. Prior-gen indoor condensing units put the same 21-ft threshold on dip switch #1 of the tan bank — confirm ON/OFF orientation in that model's manual.

Descale / Flush Procedure

Rinnai's water spec is total hardness under 200 mg/L (~12 gpg). Most of SoCal runs harder than that — flush annually and push scale treatment on every install.

  1. Power off, gas off, close the hot and cold isolation valves.
  2. Connect the pump and hoses to the service valves; drop both in a bucket with ~4 gallons of food-grade white vinegar or citric descaler.
  3. Open both service ports and circulate 45–60 minutes (SENSEI RX: use Quick-Flush mode).
  4. Dump the solution and rinse with fresh water for about 5 minutes.
  5. Pull and rinse the cold-inlet filter screen — towel down first.
  6. Return valves to service, restore power and gas, run hot water, confirm no codes.

Annual Maintenance Checklist

The visit that prevents the 2 a.m. no-hot-water call.

  • Descale / flush the heat exchanger — non-negotiable in hard water
  • Clean the cold-inlet water filter
  • Condensing units: clear the condensate trap, check neutralizer media
  • Inspect the vent run and termination screen
  • Verify inlet gas pressure at max fire
  • Pull the error-code history from the controller
